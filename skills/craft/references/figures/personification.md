Reach for it when you want a concept to feel like a force the reader has to deal with, not a topic they can deprioritize — "debt accrues" is a fact; "debt charges interest" is a creditor at the door. Avoid when the personification distorts the actual mechanics; don't say "the market wants" when you mean "three specific customers asked." # Personification

**Definition.** Giving human qualities — agency, intent, voice — to an abstract noun or object.

**Example.**
- Wrong: There are significant ongoing costs associated with technical debt.
- Good: Technical debt charges interest. Every sprint we ignore it, the bill grows.
