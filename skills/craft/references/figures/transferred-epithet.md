# Transferred Epithet

**Definition.** An adjective applied to the wrong noun — the wrong noun in the same sentence — letting the modifier slide from its logical target to a nearby object.

**Example.**
- Wrong: I had a sleepless night worrying about the launch.
- Good: The launch gave me a nervous keyboard and a sleepless inbox.
