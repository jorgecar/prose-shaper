# Rhetorical Question

**Definition.** A question posed for effect, not to elicit an answer — the writer either supplies the answer themselves or relies on the reader to supply the obvious one.

**Example.**
- Wrong: We should consider whether this roadmap is realistic.
- Good: Is this roadmap realistic? No — and pretending otherwise costs us another quarter.
