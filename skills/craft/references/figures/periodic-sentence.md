# Periodic Sentence

**Definition.** A long sentence that withholds its main clause until the end, forcing the reader through the setup before arriving at the point.

**Example.**
- Wrong: We pick Vendor B because of cost, integration, and support quality.
- Good: After six weeks of evaluation, four vendor calls, two failed proofs of concept, and one weekend reading their security audit end to end, we pick Vendor B.
