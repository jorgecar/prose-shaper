# Flow — Before/After Examples

---

## 1. Rhythm Fix

**Before**

> The system is slow. The team is frustrated. The data is unreliable. The process needs improvement. Everyone agrees something has to change.

**After**

> The system is slow, the data is unreliable, and the team has run out of patience. Something has to change — and everyone knows it.

**What changed:** Five consecutive short sentences of identical length created a staccato, list-like monotony. The rewrite merges the three evidence clauses into one compound sentence, then delivers the verdict as a short punchy close. The emphasis lands on "knows it" — the fact that nothing is disputed — rather than the generic "change."

---

## 2. Verdict → Frame Conversion

**Before**

> Remote standups are inefficient. The current format wastes time. Team engagement is low. We need a better approach.

**After**

> Every morning, twelve people dial in for thirty minutes. Ten minutes go to status updates that are already in the tracker. Four people speak; the rest watch. We have a coordination problem — not a communication one.

**What changed:** Four verdict sentences ("inefficient", "wastes time", "low", "need a better approach") were replaced with observable evidence: the headcount, the duration, the overlap with existing tools, who actually speaks. The reader reaches the diagnosis herself. The final sentence reframes the conclusion with a specific distinction instead of a generic call to action.

---

## 3. Parallel Structure

**Before**

> Good documentation saves time during onboarding, it is helpful for debugging, and the maintenance of systems becomes easier with it.

**After**

> Good documentation cuts onboarding time, shortens debugging cycles, and reduces maintenance cost.

**What changed:** The original mixed three grammatical forms for three parallel items: a subject-verb clause, a passive predicate adjective, and a nominalization with a dangling prepositional phrase. The rewrite aligns all three as verb phrases sharing the same subject ("Good documentation"). Each item now lands with equal weight, and the parallel form signals that the three benefits are comparable in kind.
